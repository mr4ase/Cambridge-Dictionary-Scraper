run-out
=======Definition 1:=======
B1 to finish, use, or sell all of something, so that there is none left:  
=======Examples of using run-out:=======
I've run out of milk/money/ideas/patience. "Do you have any milk?" "Sorry, I've run out." 


=======Definition 2:=======
B2 If a supply of something runs out, all of it has been used or it is completely finished:  
=======Examples of using run-out:=======
The milk has run out. My patience is beginning to run out. 


@call-off
=======Definition 1:=======
 to order a dog, or sometimes a person, to stop attacking someone or something:  
=======Examples of using call-off:=======
I shouted to him to call his dog off, but he just laughed at me. Call off your thugs, and I'll show you where the money is. 


=======Definition 2:=======
B2 to decide that a planned event, especially a sports event, will not happen, or to end an activity because it is no longer useful or possible:  
=======Examples of using call-off:=======
Tomorrow's match has been called off because of the icy weather. The police have called off the search for the missing child until dawn tomorrow.
